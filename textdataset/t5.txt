Mahendra Singh Dhoni, commonly known as MS Dhoni, is a former Indian international cricketer and one of the most iconic figures in the history of Indian cricket. Born on July 7, 1981, in Ranchi, Jharkhand, Dhoni is renowned for his exceptional leadership skills, cool demeanor under pressure, and prowess as a wicketkeeper-batsman.

Dhoni made his international debut for India in December 2004 in a One Day International (ODI) match against Bangladesh. He quickly rose to prominence with his aggressive batting style and lightning-fast wicketkeeping skills. He became the captain of the Indian cricket team in limited-overs formats in 2007 and later took over the Test captaincy as well.

Under Dhoni's captaincy, India achieved numerous milestones, including winning the inaugural ICC T20 World Cup in 2007, the ICC Cricket World Cup in 2011, and the ICC Champions Trophy in 2013. He is the only captain in cricket history to have won all three ICC trophies.

Dhoni's leadership style, characterized by his calm demeanor and astute decision-making, earned him the nickname "Captain Cool" among fans and cricket pundits. His finishing abilities as a batsman, particularly in high-pressure situations, made him one of the most feared and respected players in the world.

In addition to his success in international cricket, Dhoni has also been a key player in the Indian Premier League (IPL), captaining the Chennai Super Kings (CSK) franchise to multiple IPL titles.

Dhoni announced his retirement from international cricket on August 15, 2020, marking the end of an illustrious career that spanned over 16 years. Despite retiring from international cricket, Dhoni continues to be involved in the sport, notably as the captain of the Chennai Super Kings in the IPL and in various mentoring and advisory roles. He remains an iconic figure in Indian cricket and continues to enjoy immense popularity among cricket fans worldwide.
